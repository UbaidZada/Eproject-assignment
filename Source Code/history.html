K2

K2, Chinese Qogir Feng, also called Mount Godwin Austen,called locally Dapsang or Chogori, the world's second highest peak (28,241 feet [8,611 meters]), second only to mount Everst.K2 is located in the Karakoram Range and lies partly in a Chinese-administered enclave of the Kashmir Region within the Uygur Autonomous Region of Xinjiang,China and partly in the Gilit-Baltistan portion of kashmir Under the administration of Pakistan.

The Glacier-and snow-covered mountain rises from its base at about 15,000 feet (4,570) on the Godwin Austen Glacier, a tributary of the Baltaro Glacier. The mountain was discovered in 1856 by Col. T.G. Montogorie of the Survey of India, and it eas given the symbol K2 because it was the second peak measure in the Karakoram Range. The name Mount Godwin Austen is the peak's first surveyor, Col. H.H. Godwin Austen, a 19th-century English geographer.

Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji is without a doubt symbol of Japan; you can see it everywhere from postcards to movies. This is hardly surprising given its status as an important religious landmark.

The mountain is about 60 miles to thee south-west of Tokyo on the island of Honshu and the highest point of Japan at 3776 meters. Mount Fuji is an active volcano and the the summit is a creater that has erupted many times in the past.

Mount Fuji is an important place in Japanese religion. It's often known as Fujiyama and Fuji-San(Mr Fuji). It's worshipped as a god (Kami) in japan and its volanic activity symbolises the earth, sky, and fire. Thus, plenty pilgrims make th journey to the summit of Mount Fuji either on foot or in the cable car. From the foot of the mountain to the summit, there are plenty of the shrines and hot springs warmed by the volcanic activity of Mount Fuji.

Mount Pinatubo

The world largest volcanic eruption to happen in the past 100 years was the June 15,1991, eruption of the Mount Pinatubo in the Philippines. Bursts of gas-charged magma exploded into umbrella ash clouds, hot flows of gas and ash descended the valcano's flanks and lahars swept down valleys.

After the explosive eruptions posing a more serious and lingering threat to life, property and environment were the onslaught of lahars. Within hours after the eruption, heavy rains began to wash deposits of volcanic ash and debris from the slops down into the surrounding lowlands in aint,, fast-moving mudflows.Containing 40%(by weight) volcanic ash and rocks, lahars flow faster than clearwater streams. These steaming mudflows cascade as fast as 40 miles pr hour and can travel more than 50 miles. With 90% volcanic debris, lahars move fastest and are most dsetructive. When they reach the lowlands, they have speeds of more than 20 miles pre hour and are most as much as 30 feet thick and 300 feet wide.

Krakatoa

Karakatoa is a small volcanic island in indonesia, located about 100 miles wast of Jakatra. In August 1833, the eruption of the main island of Krakota(or Krakatau) killed more than 36,000 people, making it one of the most devasting volcanic eruptions in human history.

The volcanic island known as Krakatoa is located is the Sunda Strait, between the island of java and Sumatra. At the time of its famous eruption in 1883, the region was part of the Dutch East indies; it's now part of indonesia.

A previous major eruption, likely in the fifth or sixth century A.D is thought to have created Krakatoa and two nearby islands, Lang and Verlatan, as well as the undersea caldera (volcanic crater) between them. By 1833, Krakatoa was made up of three peaks: Perboewatan, the northermost and most active; Danan in the middle; and the largest, Rakata, which formed the southern end of the island.

Kanchenjunga

Kanchenjunga, also spelled Kanchenjunga, is the third highest mountain in the world. Its summit lies at 8,586 m (28,169 feet) in a section of the Himalya, the Kanchenjuga Himal which is bounded un the west by the Tamur River, in the north by the Lhanok River and Jongsang La, and in the east by the Teesta River. It lies in the border region between Nepal and Mangan district, Sikkim state of India, with three of the five peaks, namely Main, Central and South,directly on the border, and the peaks West and Kangbachen in Nepal's Taplejung District.

The Kanchenjunga is a sacred mountain in Sikkim and was first climbed on 25 May 1955 by Joe Brown and George Band, who were part of the 1955 British Kanchenjunga expedition. The Indian side of the mountain is off-limits to climbers.in 2016 the adjoining Khangchendzonga National Park was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Mayon Volcano

Mayon Valcano is the main landark and highest point of the province of Albay and the whole Bicol Region in the philippines, rising 2,463 meters (8,081 ft) from the shores of Albay Gulf about 10 kilometers (6.2 mi) away. The Valcano is geographically shared by the eight cities and municipalities of Legazpi, Daraga, Camalig, Guinobatan, ligao, Tabaco, Malilipot, and Santo Domingo (clockwise from Legazpi), which divide the cone like slices of a pie when viewing a map of their political boundries.

Moyon is the most active valcano in the philippines, erupting over 47 times in the past 500 years. Historical observation accounted its first eruption in 1616. The first eruption for which an extanded account exists was the six-day event of july 20, 1766.

Mount Elbrus

Mount El'brus is located in Southwest Russia and is part of the Caucasus mountain. It is the highest point in Russia as well as the highest point in all of Europe. It makes up part of the Prielbrusye National Park. El'brus is one of the Seven Summits of the world , which are the tallest mountains on each of the seven continents.

El'brus is an extinct vocano that is around 2.5 million years old. Its last known eruption was in 50 C.E. Ancient peoples called the mountain Strobilus which in latin means "pine cone,"due to the mountain's twisted shape.

It has two peaks, each of which rises over 5,590 meters (18,000 feet). the climate of El'brus is generally cold.Even during summer, nighttime temperatures are around -8 deg c.It is even colder above the snowline.

Mount Ararat

Mount Ararat is located in the Eastern Anotolia Region of Turkey between the provinces of Agri and Igdir, near the border with Iran,Armenia and Nakhchivan exclave of Azarbaijan, between the Aras and Murat rivers. The Serdarbulak lava plateau, at 2600 meters of elevation, seperates the peaks of Greater and little Ararat. Mount Ararat's summit is located some 16 km (10 mi) west of the turkey-Iran border and 32 km (20 mi) south of the Turco-Armenian border. the Ararat plain runs along its northwest to wester side.

Ararat is the third most prominent mountain in West Asis.

An evelation of 5,165 m (16,946 ft) for mount Ararat. The current elevation may be as low as 5,125 m (16,814 ft) due to melting of its snow-covered ice cap. The ice cap on the summit of Mount Ararat has been shrinking since at least 1957.

Nanga Parbat

A multinational Team of three climbers reached the summit of Nanga parbat in Pakistan around 3:40 p.m. on feburary 26, signifying the first time the 8,126-meter (2,660-foot) mountain has been climbed in winter. Since 1988, at least 30 different expedition comprised of some of the most experienced alpinists in the world have tried and failed to attain this coveted mountaineering prize. Ultimately, the honors of being first to climb the fearsome Nanga Parbat in the calender winter belong to simone moro of italy, Alex Txikon of spain, and ali Sadpara of Pakistan. And Sadpara has becamethe first Pakistani to achieve an 8,000-meter summit in winter.

Nanga Parbat is also unofficially reffered to as "Killer mountain," in part because at least 31 people died trying to climb it before its incredible first ascent in 1953, completed solo and without oxygen, by the legendary Austrian alpinist Hermann Buhl.
